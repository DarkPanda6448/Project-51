Camels

There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.

Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Pandas

They have great camouflage for their environment.

Only about 1,500 of these black-and-white relatives of bears survive in the wild.

Pandas eat almost nothing but bamboo shoots and leaves.

Chameleons

Almost half of all known species of Chameleons live in Madagascar.

Chameleons mainly change color to communicate or regulate body temperature.

Unlike many lizards, Chameleons can't regrow their tails.

Teddy Bears

Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.

The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff in 1903.

Up to 2,000 U.S. specialty teddy bear stores sell millions of them every year.

Cicadas

Cicadidae can be found on every continent except Antarctica

Cicadas can't eat or bite because they lack jaws

Unlike nymphs, who can live for almost 17 years, most cicadas live for about four to six weeks.

Pigeons

Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals

Both female and male pigeons share responsibility of caring for and raising young.

Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
